Causes

● Genetic Factors: Family history of anxiety disorders.

● Brain Chemistry: Imbalance in neurotransmitters like serotonin and dopamine.

● Stressful Life Events: Trauma, abuse, or significant life changes (e.g., divorce, job loss).

● Medical Conditions: Chronic illnesses, hormonal imbalances, or neurological conditions.

● Substance Abuse: Use or withdrawal from drugs, alcohol, or medications.

● Personality Traits: Tendencies toward perfectionism, low self-esteem, or sensitivity.

● Environmental Factors: High-pressure environments, financial instability, or social pressures.

Effects

● Physical Health: Increased heart rate, headaches, fatigue, and muscle tension.

● Mental Health: Difficulty concentrating, memory problems, and heightened irritability.

● Social Impact: Avoidance of social interactions, isolation, and strained relationships.

● Performance Issues: Reduced productivity, inability to focus, or absenteeism at work or school.

● Sleep Problems: Insomnia, nightmares, or disrupted sleep patterns.

● Long-term Risks: Development of chronic conditions like hypertension, heart disease, or depression.
