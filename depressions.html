Depression

Causes

● Genetic Factors: Family history of depression or mood disorders.

● Biological Factors: Imbalances in brain chemicals (e.g., serotonin, norepinephrine).

● Trauma and Stress: Past abuse, neglect, or major life changes (e.g., loss of a loved one).

● Medical Conditions: Chronic illness, hormonal imbalances, or neurological disorders.

● Substance Abuse: Alcohol, drugs, or medication misuse.

● Negative Thinking Patterns: Persistent pessimism or self-criticism.

● Environmental Factors: Social isolation, poverty, or lack of support systems.

Effects

● Emotional Impact: Persistent sadness, hopelessness, or irritability.

● Cognitive Difficulties: Problems with memory, focus, or decision-making.

● Physical Symptoms: Fatigue, changes in appetite or weight, and sleep disturbances.

● Social Withdrawal: Avoiding friends, family, or social activities.

● Work/School Impact: Decline in performance, absenteeism, or lack of motivation.

● Increased Risk of Comorbidity: Higher chances of anxiety, substance abuse, or self-harm.
● Long-term Consequences: Chronic health problems, strained relationships, or suicidal thoughts/actions.
